# Senior Biostatistician - Clinical Research

**Position Type:** Full-Time, Contract-to-Hire (18-24 months potential conversion)

---

## About the Role

We're seeking an experienced biostatistician to join our contract research organization's growing statistical sciences team. This is a unique hybrid position that bridges clinical operations and data analytics, requiring someone who can move fluidly between protocol design consultation, interim analysis execution, and regulatory submission support.

Your typical week won't look the same twice. One day you're advising a study team on sample size calculations for a Phase IIb oncology trial. The next, you're troubleshooting data quality issues with site coordinators in three time zones. The week after, you're presenting findings to an FDA panel.

**Compensation:** $95,000 - $135,000 annually, based on experience and credentials. We also offer flexible work arrangements (2-3 days on-site in our Boston location), comprehensive health coverage, CME allowance ($3,500/year), and professional society membership support.

---

## Key Responsibilities

- Design statistical analysis plans (SAPs) and statistical sections of clinical trial protocols for Phase I-IV studies across multiple therapeutic areas
- Perform interim efficacy and safety analyses; prepare Data Safety Monitoring Board packages
- Execute sensitivity analyses and subgroup evaluations; document assumptions and limitations clearly
- Mentor junior statistical programmers and coordinate with biostatisticians at other contract sites
- Maintain SAS/R programming documentation; participate in validation and QA processes
- Interface directly with clients (pharma sponsors) on statistical methodology questions—communication skills matter as much as statistical rigor here

---

## Required Experience & Skills

- 5-8 years biostatistics experience in pharmaceutical development, academic medical centers, or CRO environment
- Strong SAS programming (PROC SQL, PROC MEANS, macro development minimum); R knowledge is valuable
- Demonstrated experience with clinical trial design and analysis (ICH-GCP familiarity expected)
- Master's degree in Biostatistics, Statistics, or related quantitative field
- Advanced knowledge of survival analysis, longitudinal data methods, and missing data approaches
- FDA/EMA regulatory submission experience (e.g., CTD modules, statistical reviewer interactions)

**Nice-to-Have Skills:** Experience with adaptive trial design, Bayesian methods, CDISC standards (SDTM/ADaM), SAS Enterprise Guide, Python for data wrangling.
