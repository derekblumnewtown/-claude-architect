# Senior Materials Scientist - Advanced Composites Division

**Company:** Hexcel Aerospace Materials (Portland, OR)

**Position Type:** Full-Time, Exempt

**Reports To:** VP of R&D

---

## Overview

Hexcel is seeking a Senior Materials Scientist to lead development of next-generation carbon fiber reinforced polymer (CFRP) systems for commercial and defense aerospace applications. This role bridges fundamental materials research with manufacturing scale-up, requiring both technical depth and cross-functional leadership.

The successful candidate will establish material specifications, conduct failure mode analysis, and mentor junior researchers while maintaining publication standards for peer-reviewed journals.

## Key Responsibilities

- Design and execute experimental protocols for resin matrix formulations, fiber-matrix interface characterization, and composite mechanical testing (tensile, compression, fatigue, impact)
- Lead 3-5 person research team on assigned projects; conduct performance reviews and professional development planning
- Collaborate with manufacturing engineers to translate lab discoveries into production processes; troubleshoot scale-up challenges
- Prepare technical reports, patent documentation, and quarterly presentations for executive stakeholders
- Participate in industry standards committees (ASTM, SAE) and represent company at technical conferences
- Maintain and calibrate equipment including DMA, TGA, SEM, and universal testing machines
- Respond to customer technical inquiries; conduct failure investigations on fielded components

## Required Qualifications

- Ph.D. in Materials Science, Chemical Engineering, Polymer Science, or related field
- 7+ years of industry experience in composite materials development or aerospace manufacturing
- Proficiency with MATLAB, statistical software (JMP preferred), and materials characterization techniques
- Strong written and oral communication skills; ability to explain complex science to non-technical audiences
- Active U.S. security clearance or ability to obtain one

## Preferred Qualifications

- Experience with thermoset and thermoplastic matrix systems
- Familiarity with design of experiments (DOE) methodology
- Publication record in peer-reviewed journals
- Project management experience; exposure to aerospace quality standards (AS9100, SAE AMS)
- Knowledge of carbon fiber manufacturing and recycling technologies

## Compensation & Benefits

Salary: $145,000 - $185,000 DOE

- Comprehensive health/dental/vision coverage (company covers 85% of premiums)
- 401(k) with 6% match, vested immediately
- 22 days PTO, 10 holidays, sabbatical program after 10 years
- Tuition reimbursement ($5,000/year)
- On-site fitness center, subsidized cafeteria

## Physical Requirements

Ability to lift 50 lbs occasionally; frequent standing/walking in lab environment. Lab coat, safety glasses, and steel-toed boots required. Some travel to customer sites (10-15% annually).
