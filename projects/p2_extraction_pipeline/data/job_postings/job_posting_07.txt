# Senior Materials Scientist - Advanced Polymers Division

**Company:** Nexus Advanced Materials Corp
**Location:** Wilmington, DE 19801
**Employment Type:** Full-Time, Hybrid (3 days on-site)
**Posted:** March 15, 2024

## About Us

Nexus Advanced Materials Corp specializes in developing next-generation polymer compounds for aerospace, automotive, and medical device applications. We've been at the forefront of materials innovation for over 25 years, with R&D facilities across North America and Europe.

## Position Overview

We're seeking a Senior Materials Scientist to lead our polymer characterization and development initiatives. This role sits at the intersection of theoretical materials science and practical industrial application, requiring someone comfortable both in the lab and in cross-functional meetings with engineering teams.

## Key Responsibilities

- Design and execute experiments to evaluate mechanical, thermal, and chemical properties of novel polymer formulations
- Develop standard operating procedures for material testing protocols and maintain compliance with ASTM and ISO standards
- Mentor 2-3 junior scientists and oversee their project timelines and deliverables
- Collaborate with process engineering to transition lab-scale discoveries into pilot production
- Present findings to stakeholders including clients in defense and medical sectors
- Contribute to 2-3 peer-reviewed publications annually
- Identify emerging material trends and competitive threats in the polymers space

## Required Qualifications

PhD in Materials Science, Chemical Engineering, or related field. Minimum 8 years post-doctoral experience in polymer characterization or development. Proficiency with DSC, TGA, FTIR, and mechanical testing equipment. Experience writing technical reports and presenting to non-technical audiences. Comfortable with basic statistics and data analysis in Excel or Python.

## Preferred Qualifications

Published research in polymer blends or composites. Experience with regulatory submissions (FDA, FAA, or automotive standards). Familiarity with injection molding or extrusion processes. Project management certification or equivalent demonstrated experience. Background in sustainable/bio-based materials development.

## Compensation & Benefits

Salary range: $145,000 - $175,000 based on experience. Comprehensive health, dental, and vision insurance. 401(k) matching up to 6%. Professional development budget of $5,000 annually. Flexible work arrangements and parental leave (16 weeks primary caregiver). On-site fitness center and subsidized cafeteria.
